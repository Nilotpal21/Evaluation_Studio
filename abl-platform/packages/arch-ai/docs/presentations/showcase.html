The way it works today

Building AI agents feels like wiring a city by hand.

Every team starts from scratch. Specs, prompts, tool integrations, routing, fallbacks — all hand-crafted, all out of sync within a week.

1

Weeks of YAML and prompt-tweaking

Every agent is a hand-written file. Every change breaks something. Engineers spend more time on plumbing than product.

2

The spec is never the truth

What product wrote, what design approved, what engineering shipped — three different things. By month two, no one knows which is current.

3

You can't demo until it's "done"

Without a mock server, the only way to show the agent is to wait until every integration is real. So feedback comes too late.

The Arch AI way

Three steps. No code.

You don't pick a model. You don't write prompts. You don't draft a topology. You have a conversation, and Arch AI does the rest — bringing the right specialist in for each step.

01

Talk it out.

Describe what you want in plain English. The Onboarding specialist asks the right follow-up questions to capture intents, channels, and tone.

02

Watch it design.

The Architect proposes a topology — agents, hand-offs, build order. The Construct Expert generates working code. You stay in the conversation.

03

Ship it.

Approve and create. You walk away with a spec, a topology, compiled agents, a runnable mock server, and a decision journal — all in sync.
